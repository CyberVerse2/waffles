# Waffles — Design Context

This document is the source of truth for visual + UX direction. It guides every
design decision (`/impeccable craft`, `/impeccable shape`, hand-edits). When in
doubt, return to the **Design Principles** section at the bottom.

## Users

Real-time multiplayer trivia inside the World App, played in **micro-sessions**
on a phone — daytime breaks, commutes, waiting in line, lunch lines. Sessions
are short (~90s for a tournament round). Joy and frustration both arrive fast.

Three personas, weighted equally:

- **Crypto-native World App power users** — already comfortable with wallets,
  here for prizes and leaderboard placement, fluency with onchain mechanics.
- **Mainstream mobile gamers** (HQ Trivia / Kahoot energy) — casual phone
  gamers who happen to be in World App, here for the rush. Wallet is plumbing.
- **Global Gen-Z, mostly emerging markets** — younger users in regions where
  World App grew fastest (India, LATAM, SEA). Smaller prize amounts are
  meaningful. Visual literacy comes from TikTok / Instagram, not desktop apps.

Implication: design must read clearly on a small bright screen in a noisy
context, not signal "this isn't for you" to any of the three groups, and avoid
language or aesthetics that need crypto fluency to decode.

## Brand Personality

**Warm. Weird. Handmade.**

Sounds like: the friend who runs the local trivia night and keeps a notebook of
weird facts. Has opinions. Has a sense of humor. Confident enough to surprise.

NOT: loud-and-fast hype. NOT: premium-glossy. NOT: corporate-restrained.

Emotional goals:
- "How was this made?" not "which AI made this?"
- Calm authority during the question (no manic timer — clear pressure, not noise)
- Surprise + delight on prize moments (a single confident gesture, not fireworks)

## Aesthetic Direction

**Warm cream paper meets 70s game show meets risograph zine.**

Imagine a beautifully printed paperback game-show guide on warm cream stock —
halftone illustration, bold editorial display type, two-color print accents,
slightly imperfect rules and stamps. The interface should feel like a physical
object you'd own, not an app you're using.

**Theme: Light only.** Cream surface is canonical. (Auto-themed dark mode is a
future possibility but not the brand expression.)

### References

- **Are.na** — typographic restraint, editorial pacing, generous margins
- **NYT Magazine** + **NYT Cooking** — confident type on warm grounds
- **Penguin Classics** + 1970s mass-market paperback covers — bold display + cream
- **Risograph posters** (Colorcubic, Issue Press) — halftone, two-color, slight imperfection
- **Tom Sachs / bricolage** — stamping, masking-tape labels, handmade craft signals
- **Mast Brothers / Aesop / Pop's Books** — packaging that signals "made by people"

### Anti-references (what we DO NOT do)

- **Generic Web3 / crypto dashboard** — no purple-to-blue gradients, no glowing
  cyan accents on dark, no glassmorphism, no "data dashboard for vaporwave."
- **Generic SaaS / Linear-clone restraint** — no two-greys-and-a-blue, no
  productivity-tool minimalism. This is a game with personality, not Asana.
- **Universal AI tells** — no gradient text, no card-grid templating, no
  rounded-rectangle drop shadows for "depth," no left-stripe accent borders,
  no glassmorphism backgrounds.

## Color System (OKLCH, perceptually uniform)

The 60-30-10 split is enforced by visual *weight*, not pixel count. Cream
dominates as canvas. Ink does the type heavy lifting. Brick red stamps the
moments that matter. Ochre is a quiet supporter.

| Token              | OKLCH                          | Role                                              |
|--------------------|--------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| `--paper`          | `oklch(96.5% 0.018 80)`        | Dominant surface. Warm cream paper. (~60%)        |
| `--paper-deep`     | `oklch(93%   0.022 78)`        | Layering, cards. One step toward cardstock.       |
| `--paper-shadow`   | `oklch(89%   0.024 75)`        | Deeper edge tint for inset moments.               |
| `--ink`            | `oklch(20%   0.015 60)`        | Type, rules, marks. Warm near-black. (~30%)       |
| `--ink-mute`       | `oklch(46%   0.018 65)`        | Secondary type, captions.                         |
| `--ink-faint`      | `oklch(64%   0.014 70)`        | Tertiary type, hairlines.                         |
| `--brick`          | `oklch(54%   0.180 28)`        | Primary accent. Actions, prize, current. (~8%)    |
| `--brick-deep`     | `oklch(46%   0.190 26)`        | Pressed / hover variant of brick.                 |
| `--ochre`          | `oklch(74%   0.140 78)`        | Secondary accent. Halftone fills, complete. (~2%) |
| `--ochre-deep`     | `oklch(64%   0.150 76)`        | Pressed variant.                                  |

Neutrals are tinted toward warm hue 60-80 (paper / cardstock palette), not
toward blue. Even at the lightest cream and the darkest ink, chroma stays
small (~0.015-0.022) so the warmth reads without going garish.

**Texture rules:**
- Paper grain noise on every page surface (~3-5% opacity, multiply blend).
- Halftone dot fills on illustrations and stamp marks — not on text.
- Borders are 1px ink, sometimes intentionally varied stroke for handmade feel.
- **NO** linear/radial gradients on backgrounds, text, or buttons.
- **NO** drop shadows for depth — depth comes from layering cream tones.
- **NO** glow effects.

## Typography

**Display: [Funnel Display](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Funnel+Display)**
(Google Fonts, variable, free)

**Body: [Funnel Sans](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Funnel+Sans)**
(Google Fonts, variable, free)

Designed as a pair by Brian Beaufrere. Organic curves, optical-size axis,
character without preciousness. Not in any anti-pattern reflex list.

### Modular scale (fixed `rem` for app UI per principle)

| Token            | Size      | Use                                              |
|------------------|-----------|--------------------------------------------------|
| `--text-display` | 3rem (48) | Hero hype moments — prize won, headline reveal   |
| `--text-h1`      | 2.25rem (36) | Page headers, big editorial moments            |
| `--text-h2`      | 1.5rem (24) | Section titles, the question itself             |
| `--text-h3`      | 1.125rem (18) | Card titles, prominent labels                  |
| `--text-body`    | 1rem (16)  | Primary reading, answer text                    |
| `--text-small`   | 0.875rem (14) | Secondary reading, meta info                  |
| `--text-caption` | 0.75rem (12) | Labels, footers, captions                      |
| `--text-tag`     | 0.6875rem (11) | All-caps chips, +1px tracking                |

Weight discipline:
- **400** Funnel Sans for body
- **500** for emphasis within body
- **700+** Funnel Display for headlines
- **900** for hero moments
- Skip 600 — it reads as "trying."

Line-height:
- Tight on headlines (1.05-1.10) — display is meant to feel composed.
- Generous on body (1.55) — paper-style readability.

## Visual Marks

**Wally returns, redrawn.** New Wally is a **flat hand-illustrated waffle
character** — single-color brick-red line drawing on cream, occasionally filled
with halftone ochre. Has expressions but **does not bounce**. He sits, watches,
occasionally gestures. Replaces the bouncy pixel mascot entirely.

**Category marks** — small editorial spot illustrations per topic (tape
recorder for Music, globe for Geography, film reel for Movies, beaker for
Science, etc.). Hand-drawn line work, single color, riso-print feel. Used as
section markers, never as decoration.

**Stamps + rules** — hand-drawn-feeling 1px ink rules separate sections.
Brick-red "STAMP" marks (slightly rotated, slightly imperfect, halftone fill)
mark prize moments and key state changes — the only place rotation is allowed.

## Motion

Considered, sparing, never bouncy.

- **Page entrances**: type and surfaces rise with `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)` (ease-out-quint), 320-400ms, staggered for hierarchy.
- **Answer reveal**: subtle paper-flip or quick crossfade — no shake, no flash, no glow pulse.
- **Prize celebration**: a single brick-red stamp impacts on cream with a slight rotation and paper-grain wobble. No confetti. No fireworks.
- **Press feedback**: 1-2px translation, ink shadow softens. No 3D pop.
- **Reduced motion**: All decorative motion off; structural layout transitions kept.

## Spacing

4pt scale, semantic names:

| Token       | Pixels | Use                                          |
|-------------|--------|----------------------------------------------|
| `--space-3xs` | 4   | Tight icon gaps, tag inner padding             |
| `--space-2xs` | 8   | Stack within a card                            |
| `--space-xs`  | 12  | Default sibling gap                            |
| `--space-sm`  | 16  | Section internal padding                       |
| `--space-md`  | 24  | Between sections in a screen                   |
| `--space-lg`  | 32  | Major section breaks                           |
| `--space-xl`  | 48  | Hero margins, generous breathing room          |
| `--space-2xl` | 64  | Page top padding for major moments             |
| `--space-3xl` | 96  | Reserved for editorial hero only               |

Vary spacing for hierarchy. Headings get more space above them than below.
Don't apply the same padding everywhere.

## Radii

Restrained — most surfaces are square or near-square to feel like printed
matter. Pill shapes are reserved for chips/tags.

| Token        | Pixels | Use                                            |
|--------------|--------|------------------------------------------------|
| `--radius-0` | 0      | Sharp corners — paper edges, structural        |
| `--radius-1` | 4      | Subtle softening on tappable surfaces          |
| `--radius-2` | 10     | Card inner radius                              |
| `--radius-3` | 16     | Bottom sheets, large containers                |
| `--radius-pill` | 999  | Chips, tags, status pills only               |

## Design Principles

1. **Cream paper, never dark gaming.** The canvas is a warm physical object — a
   well-made paperback in your hand on a Tuesday afternoon, not a console at 2am.
2. **Type carries the brand.** Funnel Display does the talking. No glow, no
   chunk, no decoration that competes with the words.
3. **One accent, used like a stamp.** Brick red is rare and meaningful. The
   moment it appears, it matters. Don't dilute it.
4. **Texture from print, not gradient.** Halftones, paper grain, slightly
   imperfect rules. Never linear/radial gradients except the paper grain.
5. **Editorial restraint with one weird moment per screen.** Most surfaces
   stay calm. Surprise moments (prize, win, level-up) get a *single* bold
   gesture (the stamp impact, the rotated headline) — not a fireworks display.
